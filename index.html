Status: --

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple calculation used to assess whether a person has a healthy weight for their height. It's commonly used as a screening tool to categorize individuals into weight categories that may lead to health problems.

  • Underweight: BMI < 18.5
  • Normal weight: 18.5–24.9
  • Overweight: 25–29.9
  • Obesity: BMI ≥ 30

Adopt a Balanced Diet

Focus on whole foods:

  • Include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats
  • Choose nutrient-dense foods over empty calories

Avoid processed foods:

  • Limit sugary drinks and fast food
  • Reduce snacks high in salt or saturated fats
  • Practice portion control using smaller plates

Stay Physically Active

Cardiovascular exercises:

  • Walking, running, cycling, or swimming
  • Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week

Strength training:

  • Build muscle to boost metabolism
  • Include resistance exercises 2-3 times per week

Daily movement:

  • Take stairs instead of elevators
  • Walk for short distances instead of driving
  • Stay active during household chores

Avoid Crash Diets

  • Focus on sustainable, long-term changes
  • Avoid extreme diets and quick fixes
  • Set realistic goals for gradual weight loss

Understand Your Unique Needs

  • Tailor your diet and exercise to your lifestyle
  • Consider your age and medical conditions
  • Consult professionals for personalized advice
